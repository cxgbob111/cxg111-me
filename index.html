What moved forward

  • Closed several Y1 evidence gaps with an official English product presentation covering workflow scenarios, manipulation architecture, mobility, lift range, charging, navigation and indicative cycle-time claims.
  • Built a standalone Y1 executive ROI dashboard, turning the previous TCO logic into a tool that can support discovery, proposal framing and pilot economics.
  • Built a Priority Ledger interface to make sales focus, deadlines and completion status visible instead of leaving execution distributed across notes and conversations.
  • Defined clearer partner-policy guardrails for early-stage market development, official-status claims, local-language content and packaging localisation.
  • Advanced two live CRM opportunities during the week and captured a new inbound World Robot Conference interest submission through the HubSpot form.
  • Refined personal positioning around embodied-robotics commercialisation, European market entry and POC conversion.

Matrix progress

Y1 evidence / competitor matrix Upgraded The matrix is no longer dependent only on market proxies. Official product material now supports several core claims. Remaining gaps are approved EU pricing, CE and safety documents, warranty, SDK/API/ROS 2 clarity, and customer-grade deployment evidence.
ROI / TCO matrix Tool built The economic model has been converted into an interactive dashboard. It is ready for controlled internal use, but external use still requires approved price inputs and customer-specific labour, utilisation and integration assumptions.

Robotics Hub operating matrix Structure set The two-audience funnel, MVP website structure, assessment form, HubSpot mapping, POC offers and content sequence are now defined as concrete work items. The next gate is integration and publication, not further strategy design.
Partner / channel matrix Standardised A reusable policy summary now separates early market-development cooperation from official or exclusive partner status and creates clearer rules for localisation discussions.

Key decisions

  • Robotics Hub will serve two primary audiences: European buyers evaluating deployment and robotics companies seeking European market entry.
  • The first commercial offers will be two POC products: an industrial workflow pilot and a university/research evaluation pilot.
  • Bob's personal LinkedIn should carry expert judgement and field observations; the Robotics Hub page should publish reusable frameworks and POC methods; the website should convert interest into assessed opportunities.
  • No new matrix should be created unless it directly improves a live proposal, meeting, qualification decision or repeatable workflow.

Blockers and risks

  • Execution load is exceeding weekly capacity: many tasks were created, but too few were completed.
  • The website-to-HubSpot conversion loop has not yet been proven end to end.
  • Active deals do not yet have reliable next-activity dates, reducing forecast discipline.
  • Y1 still needs approved European commercial and compliance material before broad customer-facing release.
  • Parallel work on employment positioning, UBTECH sales and Robotics Hub can fragment focus unless outputs are consolidated into shared assets.
