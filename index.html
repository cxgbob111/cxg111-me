Weekly retrospective · 10 July 2026

From scattered research to reusable commercial infrastructure

Published

What moved forward

  • Converted competitor research into structured decision tools for full-size humanoids and wheeled mobile-manipulation platforms.
  • Defined a clearer European sales position: sell scoped pilot outcomes, compliance clarity, training, support, and measurable acceptance criteria—not a bare robot specification sheet.
  • Advanced the Robotics Hub from an idea toward an operating entity, with AI and repeatable digital workflows treated as foundational infrastructure.
  • Improved the lead-management direction by prioritising Europe and North America and identifying CRM data quality as an immediate scaling constraint.
  • Developed stronger event-led outreach logic around the World Robot Conference and China partner visits.

Matrix progress

Y1 / S2 competitor matrix Useful internally Competitive landscape, EU price anchors, TCO logic, customer segments, and battlecards are structured. The limiting factor is now official UBTECH evidence, not additional desk research.
DEX EU price matrix Research complete Public EU pricing bands and direct research-platform anchors are mapped. It still needs approved DEX configuration, support scope, and customer-facing quotation rules.
Lead / CRM operating matrix At risk The process logic is clearer, but record volume and invalid data are crowding out execution. Cleaning and prioritisation must precede further imports.
Robotics Hub operating matrix Foundation set Mission, testbed entry model, funding leverage, and minimum digital-team direction are defined. The next gap is one integrated execution dashboard with owners, cadence, and proof of completion.

Key decisions

  • Prioritise conversion infrastructure over expanding the research backlog.
  • Separate public EU prices, origin-market proxies, and quote-only competitors.
  • Position Y1 as a practical workflow pilot platform and DEX as a full-size research outcome platform.
  • Keep public logs free of customer names, confidential quotations, and unapproved product claims.

Blockers and risks

  • Missing approved product specifications, EU pricing, CE/safety documentation, SDK/API clarity, and demo performance data.
  • CRM capacity and duplicated or invalid records are now operational bottlenecks.
  • Too many parallel strategic threads can dilute follow-through on proposals, purchase orders, and booked meetings.
  • Research outputs will not create revenue unless converted into reusable customer-facing packages.

Next week’s three must-deliver items

  1. Ship one sales-ready Y1/DEX evidence pack.Create an approved one-page comparison, package structure, required proof checklist, and a customer discovery template. Completion means it can be used in a real meeting without rewriting.
  2. Recover control of the CRM.Remove or quarantine invalid records, focus the active database on Europe and North America, and ensure every priority opportunity has a next action, owner, and date.
  3. Turn the Robotics Hub strategy into an operating page.Complete the Executive Summary web block and connect the business model to a simple weekly execution matrix for pipeline, pilots, funding, partners, and digital coworkers.

Protected research slot

Wednesday, 09:00–12:00: evidence-gap research only. No broad competitor expansion. The output should be missing official documentation, approved claims, pilot KPIs, and the exact materials required to move live opportunities forward.
